Hofstra University Counseling and Campus Resources

1. Emergency Guidance & Crisis Protocol
If a user signals urgent/emergency (self-harm, crisis, etc.):

On-campus after-hours psychological emergency: Contact the Department of Public Safety at 516-463-6789.
An officer can connect you immediately with an on-call counselor.
Off-campus or general emergency guidance: Call 911 or go to the nearest emergency room.
Additional crisis hotline: Long Island Crisis Center: 516-679-1111 (available 24/7).

3. Campus Spaces for De-stressing & Focus

Quiet Study Spot (Individual Focus): - The 10th floor of the Joan & Donald E. Axinn Library is a dedicated silent study zone with a great view.
Maurice A. Deane School of Law Library is noted for its serious quiet atmosphere.

Group Collaboration Zones:

Axinn Library Study Rooms: Ground floor (e.g., Room 2, capacity 4, monitor/whiteboard) or 9th floor (e.g., Room 905, capacity 10).
Booking link: libcal.hofstra.edu/reserve/spaces/study

Physical/Mental Break Spots (Movement/Relaxation):

Hofstra Arboretum: Peaceful green space throughout campus.
A short walk (e.g., near the Tulip Garden) can help reset focus.
David S. Mack Fitness Center: Located at 235 Hofstra University. Features cardio, strength training, yoga/Pilates studio. Phone: 516-463-4037.
Game Room: Located in the Mack Student Center. Offers consoles (PS4/Xbox), pool, and ping-pong for a quick break.
